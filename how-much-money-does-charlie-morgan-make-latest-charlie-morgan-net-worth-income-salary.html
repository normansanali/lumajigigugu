Charlie Morgan (born John Charles Morgan in Hammersmith, London, England, on August 9, 1955) is an English drummer and percussionist. Morgan began his professional career when he was 18 years old, in 1973. By the mid-1980s, he had established himself as one of the UK’s finest session drummers. He appeared on albums by Kate Bush, Roy Harper, Judie Tzuke, Clannad, Tracey Ullman, Gary Moore, and Justin Hayward in the early 1980s. Elton John heard about his drumming work with Nik Kershaw in 1985 and hired him to play on his Ice on Fire album. Later that year, John encouraged him to perform at Live Aid with his band. This was the beginning of a thirteen-year recording and touring relationship with John. Morgan and bassist Andy Pask collaborated on the theme music for the ITV TV show The Bill. He currently resides in Nashville, Tennessee, United States of America.
