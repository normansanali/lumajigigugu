Scott D. Malkin: Businessman and NHL Co-Owner

Scott D. Malkin is an American businessman and entrepreneur with a net worth of $200 million. He is the co-owner of the NHL’s New York Islanders, which he purchased in 2014. Malkin was born in 1959 and is the son of Peter L. Malkin, the former owner of the Empire State Building in New York.

Malkin graduated with a B.A. from Harvard College, a J.D. from Harvard Law School, and an M.B.A. from Harvard Business School. He founded Value Retail Plc in 1992 and owned the retail store 2 Rodeo Drive in Beverly Hills, California. Value Retail Plc offers outlet centers on the outskirts of large cities and sells excess stock from brand name retailers.

The company has villages in Europe near nine major cities and expanded into China, with more than $1.5 billion total sales per year. Malkin’s sister, Cynthia Malkin, is married to United States Senator Richard Blumenthal.
