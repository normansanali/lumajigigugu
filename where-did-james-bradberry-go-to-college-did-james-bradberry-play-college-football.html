Where did James Bradberry go to college? Did James Bradberry play college football?

Publish date: 2024-07-19

James Bradberry, a cornerback with the National Football League’s Philadelphia Eagles, was born on August 4, 1993. (NFL).

Where did James Bradberry go to college? Did James Bradberry play college football?

The Carolina Panthers selected him in the second round of the 2016 NFL Draft after he played college football for Samford. Bradberry suffered a fractured wrist during OTAs on June 5, 2017, and was ruled out for at least a month. Head coach Ron Rivera named Bradberry and Worley the starting outside cornerbacks, with newly acquired free agent Captain Munnerlyn serving as the starting Nickelback.

Bradberry had six combined tackles, a pass breakup, and his first career sack on quarterback Carson Wentz in a 28-23 loss to the Philadelphia Eagles on October 12, 2017. In the Panthers’ 31-24 win over the Minnesota Vikings in Week 14, he had six combined tackles, a season-high three pass deflections, and intercepted Case Keenum’s pass. Bradberry had a season-high seven combined tackles, a pass deflection, and intercepted Aaron Rodgers’ pass the following week in a 31-24 victory over the Green Bay Packers. In 16 games and 16 starts, Bradberry concluded the 2017 campaign with 85 combined tackles (66 solos), eleven pass deflections, three interceptions, and a sack.  Among all qualified cornerbacks in 2017, Pro Football Focus gave Bradberry an overall rating of 42.4 and placed him at position 109.
