Overall Meaning

The lyrics to Don Toliver’s song Bus Stop are about a breakup and the emotional pain that comes with it. The artist talks about missing his ex-girlfriend and wanting to get back together with her. He also mentions a girl he sees at the bus stop who reminds him of his former lover. He wonders about what happened to her and if she also misses him. The song also has sexual undertones with lyrics about being with more than one woman and possibly having a threesome.


Don Toliver uses repetition in the chorus with the line “Oh yeah like she go low.” This repetition adds emphasis to the feelings and emotions that the artist is describing. Furthermore, the chorus establishes a hypnotic rhythm and helps convey the melancholic mood of the song. The line “Most of the room didn't make it, the kid was like” can refer to a party that didn't end well. The “kid” in the line may refer to Don Toliver himself, who may have been affected by the fallout of the party.


In conclusion, Bus Stop is a song that talks about pain, heartbreak, and sexual relationships. The lyrics are layered with complex emotions and keeps listeners intrigued. Like many of Don Toliver's songs, Bus Stop has a unique style and sound that has helped him build a dedicated fan base.
